SOCIAL STUDIES: The Three Fires Confederacy

Long before any European ship crossed the Atlantic Ocean, the Great Lakes region was home to thriving Indigenous nations. Among the most powerful was an alliance called the Three Fires Confederacy, made up of three related Anishinaabe peoples: the Ojibwe, the Odawa, and the Potawatomi.

The Ojibwe were known as the "Keepers of the Faith." They were the spiritual leaders, responsible for preserving sacred traditions and ceremonies. The Odawa earned the title "Keepers of Trade." They traveled vast distances by canoe, creating trade networks that stretched hundreds of miles. The Potawatomi were called the "Keepers of the Fire," responsible for the heart of the community.

Together, these three nations created a powerful alliance based on cooperation rather than competition.

The Area Model breaks division into friendly chunks.
For 84 / 7: First, 7 x 10 = 70 (fits inside 84). Then 84 - 70 = 14 left. Finally, 14 / 7 = 2.
