MATH: Division as the Opposite of Multiplication
Key Concept: Division and multiplication are inverse operations. If you know that 8 x 9 = 72, then you also know that 72 / 8 = 9 and 72 / 9 = 8.

SOCIAL STUDIES: Meet the Three Fires Confederacy

Long before any European ship crossed the Atlantic Ocean, the Great Lakes region was home to thriving Indigenous nations. Among the most powerful was an alliance called the Three Fires Confederacy, made up of three related Anishinaabe peoples: the Ojibwe, the Odawa, and the Potawatomi. These three nations shared similar languages, beliefs, and customs, but each had their own special role in the alliance.

The Ojibwe, sometimes called Chippewa, were known as the "Keepers of the Faith." They were the spiritual leaders of the confederacy, responsible for preserving sacred traditions, ceremonies, and the stories that passed wisdom from generation to generation. The Ojibwe lived primarily in the northern Great Lakes region, in areas that are now Michigan, Wisconsin, and Minnesota.

The Odawa, also spelled Ottawa, earned the title "Keepers of Trade." They were master negotiators and traders who traveled vast distances by canoe, creating trade networks that stretched hundreds of miles. The Odawa traded copper, furs, corn, and other goods between different nations.

The Potawatomi were called the "Keepers of the Fire," which was a sacred responsibility. Fire represented the heart of the community—the place where people gathered, where food was prepared, and where important decisions were made. The Potawatomi lived in the southern Great Lakes region and were known for their farming skills.

Together, these three nations created a powerful alliance based on cooperation rather than competition. When one nation faced a threat, the others came to help. When one nation had extra food, they shared with the others.

7. The passage says the Three Fires Confederacy was based on "cooperation rather than competition." What evidence from the passage supports this? 4-H3.0.2

MATH: The Area Model for Division
The Area Model breaks division into friendly chunks. For 84 / 7:
Step 1: Find a friendly multiple of 7 that fits inside 84 → 7 x 10 = 70 (that fits!)
Step 2: Subtract and see what's left → 84 - 70 = 14
Step 3: Divide the leftover → 14 / 7 = 2
Step 4: Add your partial quotients → 10 + 2 = 12
Answer: 84 / 7 = 12
